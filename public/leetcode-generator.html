How to Ace Technical Interviews

Problem Solving Approach

Start by clarifying the problem, identify edge cases, think of a brute force solution, then optimize. Always explain your thought process - this is as important as the solution itself.

Time Management

Allocate time effectively: ~5 minutes for understanding the problem, ~15-20 minutes for solution development, and ~5 minutes for testing. Practice with a timer to build this skill.

Communication

Think out loud and explain your approach before coding. Use clear variable names and add comments. Ask for clarification when needed and be receptive to hints.

Testing & Debugging

After coding, trace through your solution with a simple example. Look for edge cases: empty inputs, single elements, duplicates, etc. Fix bugs methodically.

Consistent Practice

Practice daily with a mix of problems. Focus on understanding patterns rather than memorizing solutions. Review and optimize previous solutions regularly.

Pattern Recognition

Learn to recognize common patterns like two pointers, sliding window, BFS/DFS, dynamic programming, etc. This helps tackle new problems by applying known strategies.
